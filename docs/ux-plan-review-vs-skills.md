# UX-Quality Plan Review — against the new motion/design skills

*Prepared for the UPR owner · 2026-07-13 · scope: the F-S2 motion foundation, `.claude/rules/motion-standard.md`, `docs/ux-quality-roadmap.md`/`dispatch.md`, and the six craft skills (apple-design, emil-design-eng, review-animations, improve-animations, impeccable, vercel-*) plus playwright-core and supabase-postgres-best-practices.*

---

## 1. Verdict

**The plan holds up — it does not need a rethink — but it needs a real adjustment, and the honest gap is not "wrong foundation," it's "missing craft layer + one un-made decision + one structural blocker."** The foundation you already shipped (directional View Transitions in `App.jsx`/`TechLayout.jsx` + `index.css:11516-11551`, the base `.btn:active{scale(0.97)}` press, the `--motion-*` token block, the reduced-motion wrapper) is *exactly* what all six skills prescribe as the achievable core — ~90% of the craft bar is reachable on CSS transitions + View Transitions + tokens, and the framer-motion ban costs almost nothing across that 90%. Keep it. The problem is that the rollout optimizes for **uniformity** (right token, no raw literal, `design-consistency-checker` + the eslint ratchet) and never applies the thing that actually separates *consistent* from *amazing*: a per-interaction frequency/purpose pass, a motion feel-gate, the named high-craft-per-line CSS idioms, and one deliberate decision about the 10% of the bar (gesture + spring) that CSS genuinely cannot express.

**The single blocker.** Of everything below, exactly one finding is **blocker-severity** (the only blocker across all four audits): the mandatory close-out has **no motion feel-gate** — the *uniformity* gates are CI-enforced while `review-animations` sits as advisory prose. That asymmetry is the structural reason the app converges on *consistent* and stops. It is not a co-equal of the ten prioritized items; it is the precondition for them holding (see §3 lead-in and §4). Two other items are **decisions only you can make** (springs/gestures; whether to pull DB indexing forward); the rest are **do-its** that slot onto the existing base without reworking it.

## 2. The central decision — springs & gestures

This is the one fork worth your attention. Every skill pack converges on the same boundary, and it is a real ceiling, not a taste dispute.

**What CSS + View Transitions + WAAPI can do (keep as-is):** entrances/exits, directional page push + shared-element continuity, origin-anchored menus, press feedback, `@starting-style` interruptible toasts, clip-path fills, staggered lists, materialized glass, a *one-shot* spring settle, and — via **WAAPI `element.animate()`** — any *dynamic, JS-computed one-shot* (a value known only at runtime) without touching a library. All zero-dep, all iOS-Safari-supported. (WAAPI is the sanctioned dep-free escape hatch — see #6/#9 — so the framer-motion ban never means "no JS motion at all.")

**What they genuinely cannot do (the ceiling):** 1:1 finger-tracked drag from the grab offset, **velocity handoff** on release, **grab-and-reverse** interruptibility, **momentum projection** on a flick, and custom rubber-banding. These need Pointer Events + a velocity-aware spring. A CSS transition always restarts from zero velocity over a fixed duration — faking momentum with a long transition reads *dead*, which is worse than not shipping it. This caps your bottom sheets (`PhotoNoteSheet`, `ClockSupersedeSheet`, the mobile `.ui-modal` @768px), `PullToRefresh.jsx`, and any swipe-to-dismiss at "good, not alive."

### The three options

- **(A) Stay CSS/View-Transitions only.** Zero new code, stays well inside the perf budget. Sheets pop out of existence (there is currently *no exit animation at all* — see §3), pull-to-refresh stays canned. On iOS this reads subtly un-native on exactly the surface your gloved-tech persona touches most physically.
- **(B) Add a general spring/gesture library** (Framer Motion ~40KB+ gz, Motion One, react-spring). **Rejected.** `perf-budget.md` caps the entry graph at **232 KB gz** and your reference scenario is LTE cold-start; a global animation lib on an internal field-tool for a 64-yo-in-gloves is over-engineering, and impeccable/Emil's "product register" explicitly values crisp reliability over consumer-grade spring bounce.
- **(C · the middle path) A tiny, dependency-free pointer+rAF spring util (~1–2 KB), scoped to ≤3 surfaces**, route-lazy. `useRef` → write `node.style.transform` per frame (never `useState` — the vercel rule), `setPointerCapture`, velocity from a short pointer history, dismiss on `|dist|/elapsed > ~0.11`, hand the settle/fling-out to a CSS transition.

### Recommendation

**Adopt (C), tightly scoped to the mobile bottom-sheet drag-to-dismiss + `PullToRefresh` + swipe-to-dismiss toast — and nothing else.** Everywhere else, lean on native overflow scroll (which gives momentum + rubber-banding *for free*) and cut any non-native swipe affordance rather than ship a canned one. This is the single place physical drag feel pays for itself, the util is small and lazy, and it stays inside 232 KB. Do **not** touch the global lib ban.

**Reconcile the gesture code that already ships — this is not greenfield.** Two live surfaces already carry drag: the **CrmLeads kanban** (ad-hoc touch-drag) and the **dashboard widgets** on **`react-grid-layout ^2.2.3`** (a gesture-capable dep the app *already* bundles, which gives free momentum/rubber-band for its draggables). The scoped-util phase must (a) prefer `react-grid-layout`'s built-in behavior for anything grid-like rather than reinventing it, and (b) explicitly **either fold CrmLeads' ad-hoc drag into the new util or consciously leave it as-is with a note** — not pretend the ≤3 target surfaces are the only gesture code in the app.

### The free zero-dep wins you're leaving on the table right now — ship regardless of the (C) decision

These are **not** part of option C; they ship whether or not you approve the pointer/rAF util.

- **`--motion-spring-in: linear()` token.** `src/index.css` has **zero `linear()` usage**. Apple takeaway #4 + Emil: bake a spring curve into a **`--motion-spring-in: linear(...)`** token and you capture ~90% of "springy" for any *non-interruptible one-shot* (modal pop, toast in, menu Pop-in) with **no JS at all**. Add it to the `:root` catalog, point `uiModalIn`/menu enter/toast at it, keep it *off* money/claims surfaces (cohesion rule) and off the sheet if the sheet becomes drag-interruptible. Pure CSS, zero risk, ships independent of §2's springs/gestures fork.
- **Shared-element (Continuity) list→detail transition.** The tier above the whole-screen push: a named `view-transition-name` on the tapped row growing into the detail header, scoped to Jobs/Claims/Conversations. Zero-dep, View-Transitions-native, degrades gracefully. Sequence it as a later (non-gate) item on the Motion phase (#9), not a gate — but it's on the table now and shouldn't be lost.

**Perf/bundle reality:** (C) adds ~1–2 KB lazy; the `linear()` token adds bytes to CSS only. Framer Motion (B) would blow ~17% of the JS budget (~40KB / 232KB) on an app whose flagship metric is LTE boot. The math makes (C) + `linear()` the only defensible answer.

## 4. How we USE the skills going forward

Wire them into the workflow so craft is enforced, not hoped for. (All rule/checker/`close-out-standard.md` edits below route through the **F-S1/Foundation** owner of those frozen files — a W-session cannot land them.)

- **`review-animations` → the mandatory close-out GATE (the §1 blocker fix).** Promote it into `close-out-standard.md`'s gauntlet, beside `design-consistency-checker`, for any PR touching motion/transitions. Today it's advisory prose in `motion-standard.md §8` while the *uniformity* gates are CI-enforced — that asymmetry is exactly why the app converges on consistent and stops, and it is the single blocker-severity finding across the audits. It runs the keep-or-delete + purpose-per-animation + easing/duration/origin checks before merge.
- **`improve-animations` → authors each wave's motion plan.** Before a wave touches motion, run it read-only to produce the recon → frequency-map → severity-ranked findings → self-contained per-fix plans (exact `file:line`, verbatim current code, target token value, feel-check). This is how the Motion phase (#9) gets its work list instead of a vague "animate the indicators."
- **`apple-design` / `emil-design-eng` / `impeccable` → build-time references** (not gates). The engineer opens them while implementing the #5 idioms and the depth/materials/typography polish — they answer "what's the correct transform-origin / tracking / vibrancy contrast," not "did it pass."
- **`playwright-core` → the objective motion/UI verification gate that replaces "does it feel right" guessing.** Four axes, all CI-runnable: **end-state web-first assertions** prove a transition *fired* (`toBeVisible`/`.not.toBeVisible` retries through the exit — never `waitForTimeout`); an **rAF FPS probe under 4× CPU throttle** asserts ≥55fps on the route push + one sheet (the 60fps floor); **`toHaveScreenshot({animations:'disabled'})` + `toHaveCSS`** catch token/theme/dark-mode drift (pin `transition-duration`/easing/color values — cheaper than pixels for a token app); and **`emulateMedia({reducedMotion:'reduce'})` + axe-core** prove compliance (motion collapses to instant, end-state still lands, focus-trap/aria-live intact). Mock the Supabase RPCs (`page.route`) and freeze `page.clock` for determinism, and run the matrix on **Desktop Chrome + iPhone-14 WebKit (390px) + Pixel 7**. **Caveat to state plainly:** Playwright runs Chromium in CI — it verifies *behavior and regressions*, **not the true iOS-Safari/WKWebView feel**. Gesture feel, real haptics, and the actual 60fps-scroll-under-blur question (#11) stay an **owner on-device iPhone check** (noted in the PR like the minimize test). Playwright can *simulate* the pointer sequence to verify a drag-to-dismiss lands past threshold, but it cannot tell you the fling *feels* native.
- **`supabase-postgres-best-practices` → the perceived-performance layer** (#8). Motion quality and query latency are coupled: index the WHERE/JOIN/FK columns behind hot list RPCs so the screen paints *inside* the ~200ms transition instead of flashing after it. Rank real offenders with `pg_stat_statements` (>100ms mean, high calls), EXPLAIN-ANALYZE, record the before/after in the PR perf delta. Additive-only migrations per `database-standard.md` — this is largely db-foundation P5 pulled forward for the transitioned boards.
- **A per-interaction craft pass** becomes a first-class close-out step (owned by the Motion phase): DevTools Animations panel at 10% playback, spam-the-control interruptibility, reduced-motion toggle, real-iPhone gesture/haptic check plus the #11 blur-scroll measurement.

## 5. What NOT to do

- **Don't add a general spring/animation library** (Framer Motion/GSAP/react-spring) to the entry graph. ~40KB+ gz against a 232 KB budget on an LTE field tool = the wrong trade. Scoped dep-free util only — and reuse the already-bundled `react-grid-layout` for grid drag rather than adding anything new (§2).
- **Don't chase consumer bounce/spring on core surfaces.** impeccable/Emil's product register: an operations tool reads crisp and fast. Keep overshoot subtle (0.1–0.3), and keep *any* springiness **off money/claims/billing** screens — a bouncing invoice erodes trust.
- **Don't over-motion the high-frequency loop.** A tech clocking in 40×/shift or flipping the day view constantly should wait on *nothing*. Delete-the-animation is a legitimate top fix; delight belongs at first-run/completion moments, not on every tab tap. (And ship the #2 checker change *with* the behavior, or CI fails the correct code.)
- **Don't chase 120Hz.** WKWebView caps web content at 60fps even in the Capacitor build (`CADisableMinimumFrameDurationOnPhone` only affects native Core Animation; the 120Hz-web path needs private WebKit APIs = App Store review risk). Design for a well-tuned 60fps — short durations, right easing, transform/opacity only. Nothing in the catalog may assume >60fps.
- **Don't lean on haptics for the feel.** `navigator.vibrate` is a genuine no-op on Safari *and* the installed PWA; only the Capacitor native build gets Taptic. Visual motion carries the entire experience on web — which is why easing/duration/origin discipline matters more here than the haptic tick. (Add the tick where it's free, per #4, but don't design around it.)
- **Don't ship `blur(20px)` on the tech nav without measuring it** (#11). Materialize-glass is a real Apple-feel-per-line win, but a 20px backdrop-filter over a scrolling list is a known WKWebView jank source on the exact LTE persona surface — measure on a real iPhone, drop to ~14px if it stutters. Otherwise gold-plate depth/materials (scroll-edge masks, vibrancy) only *after* the load-bearing fixes: exit animation, frequency-gating, haptics, the reduced-motion sweep.
- **Don't let Playwright's green CI imply "feels native."** It's a regression net on Chromium. The iOS feel — gesture fling, real haptics, blur-scroll — is still an owner device pass.
