How everything fits together

From the customer at the top to the bill at the bottom, every record in UPR hangs off a simple four-level chain. Read it top-to-bottom: each level can contain several of the level below it.

The chain

1

Client

The homeowner or property owner we're doing the work for. One person (or business) we have a relationship with.

contacts
▾ one client can have many claims
2

Claim

One insurance loss — a single thing that went wrong on one date (a burst pipe, a kitchen fire). It's the umbrella that holds all the work for that loss. Not a sale, not a bill.

CLM-2606-001
▾ one claim splits into many jobs
3

Job

One trade / division of the work on that loss — water mitigation, then reconstruction, then contents. Each job is exactly one division and runs on its own schedule.

WTR-2606-001
▾ each job gets exactly one invoice
4

Invoice

The bill for that one job. Built from its own line items, split between insurance and homeowner, and synced to QuickBooks under that division.

INV-001000

One client, two separate losses → two claims. The basement claim needed two trades, so it became two jobs (water + reconstruction). Every job carries one division and produces one invoice. The claim itself is just the umbrella — its "total" is simply the sum of its jobs' invoices.

The rules, in plain English

✓
A client can have many claims.Each claim is a different loss event. Sarah's pipe and her fire are two unrelated claims under the same person. claims.contact_id → contacts.id
✓
A claim is everything for one specific loss.One date of loss, one carrier claim number. It groups together all the work that came out of that single event. claims.date_of_loss
✓
A claim can have many jobs.The work is split by trade — mitigation, reconstruction, mold, contents — so each crew and each scope is tracked on its own. jobs.claim_id → claims.id
✓
A job is exactly one division.A job is never "water and reconstruction" — those are two jobs. The division decides the crew, the job-number prefix, and the QuickBooks category. jobs.division
✓
A job has one invoice.Each job is billed as its own unit. Creating an invoice for a job is idempotent — ask twice, get the same invoice. (A supplement is the rare exception that adds a second.) invoices.job_id → jobs.id
